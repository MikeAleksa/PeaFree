About

My vet recently cautioned me against grain-free diets after a recent FDA investigation into these foods, which indicates some of their main ingredients may be responsible for a rise in cases of Canine Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM), a type of heart disease in dogs. This website utilizes data scraped from Chewy.com to see which dog foods meet the FDA's new recommendations - no peas, no potatoes, no legumes, and no pulses.

The FDA's announcement can be found here and is summarized here.

Key points from the FDA's summary:

  • The FDA is cautioning against feeding dogs food that contains legumes, pulses (seeds of legumes), and/or (sweet) potatoes as main ingredients in the food

  • Main ingredients are defined as ingredients that appear in a food's ingredient list before the first appearance of a vitamin or mineral ingredient.

  • Common legumes include peas, beans, lentils, chickpeas, soybeans and peanuts. Pulses are dry edible seeds of certain legume plants. Examples include dried beans, dried peas, chickpeas and lentils.

  • Rice is a grain, not a legume. The current reports do not suggest there is any link between diets with rice and DCM in dogs.

  • Grain-free diets are not necessarily specifically implicated by the study, but recently the proportion of legumes and/or pulses has increased significantly in these diets.
